# My Rights User Guide

## Accessing My Rights

### Opening the feature
To access My Rights:

1. Open the **MyChild Helpline App**.
2. Go to the bottom navigation bar.
3. Tap **Information Kiosk**.
4. Select **My Rights**.

The app opens the My Rights feature inside the Information Kiosk.

## Introduction screen

### Helping Hand introduction
When you open My Rights, Helping Hand appears and introduces the concept of children’s rights.

Helping Hand explains that:
- Every child has rights.
- Rights are things children should have or be able to do to have the best start in life.
- These rights are based on the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child.
- They apply to all children under 18 worldwide.
- All rights are equally important and connected.
- Children are born with these rights, and no one can take them away.

### Audio playback
You can listen to the introduction by tapping **Play**.

- Tap **Play** to start the audio.
- Tap **Pause** to stop the audio.

## Getting started

### Entering the rights section
To proceed to the main content, tap **Let’s go**.

The app opens the list of children’s rights.

## Browsing children’s rights

### Viewing rights
Each right is displayed one at a time.

You can:

- Read each right on the screen.
- Listen to each right using audio playback.
- Navigate through the rights using swipe gestures.

There are a total of 41 rights.

### Navigation between rights
The feature supports simple swipe navigation:

- Swipe right to move to the next right.
- Swipe left to return to the previous right.

This allows users to easily move forward and review previous content when needed.

## Navigation controls

### Back button
To return to the previous screen:

- Tap the **“<” icon** at the upper-left corner of the screen.

## Summary
My Rights presents the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child in a simple, interactive format. Users can listen to explanations, view 41 rights individually, and navigate through content using left and right swipe gestures.
